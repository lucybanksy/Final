Values

Equality

I was raised in Arlington, VA just outside Washington, DC. Many people are brought to the area for countless reasons cultivating an incredibly diverse space. I was aware from a young age that while our differences are what make us beautiful, they are often used to tear one another down. No matter what I end up doing in my career, the most important thing to me is that I do the absolute best I am capable of to support equal and dignified treatment to my fellow person.

Compassion

"Being kind is free"
The world can be a very scary place, many of us experienced this in a whole new way during the pandemic. Despite whatever we may be feeling personally, unprompted kindness is truly priceless. While I agree children shouldn't talk to strangers, as an adult I have found a friendly stranger can make all the difference in the world.

Humility

We live in a big world that keeps getting bigger. It is easy to play the blame game and get caught up in the chaos of the modern era. This is why it's all the more important to be introspective in your part or role in society. It's great to strive for greatness, but no matter what status you may or may not have, you're only as great as you treat those around you.

The Dream

Making a difference one patient at a time

My dream is to provide care to people of all shapes and sizes. My two biggest passions in life are exercising my creativity through working in hands on projects or environments and helping others in a tangible way. Medicine is the perfect combination of these two. In my work as a medical assistant for the past three years, I have had to privilege to be apart of patients' medical journies. From fighting breast cancer to removing a stye of the eye, I have cherished every moment I am able to contribute to bettering the health of others. I also am proud to say I love my job. Many people never find their passion or hate their job. Working to live rather than living to work. I am extremely grateful to get to look forward to going to work everyday and cannot wait to see where medicine takes me next.
